# Skill: Member Archetypes

Behavioral segments used to guide intervention style and tone. These labels
are INTERNAL — never shared with members or non-GM staff.

## Archetype Definitions

### The Social Golfer
- **Pattern:** 3+ rounds/week, dines after golf frequently, brings guests regularly
- **Motivation:** Community, ritual, competitive camaraderie
- **Intervention style:** Event invitations (member-guest, captain's game, twilight league),
  recognition from the Head Pro, competitive formatting
- **Re-engagement trigger:** Invite to a format that matches their skill level

### The Family Member
- **Pattern:** Kids in programs (swim, tennis, junior golf), spouse active, weekend visits
- **Motivation:** Family destination, kids' development, social bonds with similar families
- **Intervention style:** Family-focused invitations (holiday brunch, kids' camp, family tournament),
  acknowledgment of children by name
- **Re-engagement trigger:** Event that includes children or family activity

### The Business Networker
- **Pattern:** Books private dining regularly, brings clients and prospects, hosts events
- **Motivation:** Professional network, client entertainment, prestige of the venue
- **Intervention style:** Complimentary private dining evening, exclusive access offers,
  personalized event hosting support
- **Re-engagement trigger:** New event or dining upgrade they can host clients at

### The Legacy Member
- **Pattern:** 10+ year tenure, declining visits, likely retirement age, high dues
- **Motivation:** History, belonging, identity, reputation
- **Intervention style:** Personal GM call, "we miss you" tone, acknowledge tenure milestones,
  no pressure to increase activity — validate their connection
- **Re-engagement trigger:** Something that honors their history (naming recognition, milestone event)

### The New Member
- **Pattern:** <12 months tenure, engagement variable, still establishing habits
- **Motivation:** Social integration, value realization, finding their place
- **Intervention style:** Buddy-pair with active member of similar archetype,
  new-member mixer, structured introduction to club features they haven't used
- **Re-engagement trigger:** Personal invitation from an established member in their
  interest area (golf partner, dining companion)

## Application Rules

- Use archetype to SELECT the intervention style, not to label the member in
  any external communication.
- When archetype is uncertain, default to Legacy Member norms for tenure > 5 years
  and New Member norms for tenure < 18 months.
- Archetypes can shift over time — a Business Networker may become a Social Golfer
  after retirement. Watch for pattern shifts.
