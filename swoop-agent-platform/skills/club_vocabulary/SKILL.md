# Skill: Club Vocabulary

This skill teaches agents the terminology, naming conventions, and shorthand
used at private golf and country clubs.

## Facility Terms

- **The Grill Room** (not "restaurant" or "dining room" informally)
- **The Terrace** — outdoor dining, weather-dependent
- **The Pro Shop** — golf shop, not "store"
- **The Turn** — mid-round break between holes 9 and 10
- **The Starter** — staff member managing tee time departures
- **The Range** — practice facility (not "driving range" in conversation)
- **The Locker Room** — never "locker rooms"; singular is standard usage

## Membership Terms

- **Member** — always capitalized in formal contexts
- **Guest** — a member's invited non-member
- **Social Member** — non-golf membership
- **Junior Member** — membership category, age-based
- **Legacy Member** — long-tenure member (typically 10+ years)
- **Annual dues** — not "subscription" or "fees" in member-facing copy

## Golf Operations

- **Tee time** (two words, lowercase)
- **Tee sheet** — the day's booking schedule
- **Foursome** — group of four players (default assumption)
- **Pace of play** — not "speed of play"
- **Cart path only** — weather condition instruction
- **Frost delay** — morning delay for turf protection
- **Captain's game** — member competition format

## Food and Beverage

- **Cover** — one dining guest (not "seat" or "reservation")
- **Turn bar** — the between-holes refreshment station
- **Beverage cart** — the mobile drink service on course
- **Private dining** — reserved rooms for events
- **Member charge** — billing to member account (not "tab")

## Communication Conventions

- Members are addressed by first name after the first formal greeting
- "Mr." and "Ms." only in written correspondence, not conversation
- "The Club" (capitalized) when referring to the club as an institution
- Avoid "our system," "the database," "the platform" in member-facing copy
