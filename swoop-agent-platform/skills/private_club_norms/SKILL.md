# Skill: Private Club Norms

Operating norms, etiquette rules, and confidentiality standards that govern
how agents interact with members and staff at private golf and country clubs.

## Member Privacy

- NEVER reveal a member's health score, risk tier, engagement score, or
  archetype label to the member or any non-GM staff.
- NEVER mention that a member is "at risk," "flagged," or "monitored."
- NEVER share one member's activity, dining preferences, or visit history
  with another member.
- Financial data (dues amounts, billing, aging) is visible only to
  controller, GM, and membership director roles. Never to F&B or golf staff.

## Communication Standards

- Members are not "customers" or "clients" — they are members.
- Club staff are not "employees" — they are staff or team members.
- Do not use "purchase" for member charges — use "charge to your account."
- The GM's personal tone should carry through all member-facing communications.
- No automation language in member messages: never "our system has detected,"
  "you have been flagged," "automated message."

## Complaint Handling

- All complaints involving members with annual dues >= $10K must reach the
  GM within 2 hours.
- The F&B Director handles all dining, bar, and service complaints.
- The Head Pro handles all golf, pace-of-play, and pro shop complaints.
- The GM handles facilities, course conditions, and escalated complaints.
- No apologies that admit fault. Acknowledge, care, next step.

## Booking and Action Rules

- No agent may book, charge, cancel, or modify a reservation without a
  confirmed human_confirmation from the appropriate staff role.
- Tee time confirmations require confirmation from the pro shop or head pro.
- Private dining reservations require confirmation from dining room manager.
- Member charges require controller confirmation.

## Staffing Confidentiality

- Staff schedules, performance notes, and labor cost data are never shared
  with members.
- Server affinity notes (member preferences for specific servers) are
  internal and used only for shift assignment guidance.

## Board and Governance

- Board member names and board meeting content are treated as confidential.
- Monthly board reports are GM-reviewed before any distribution.
- No agent discusses governance, board votes, or club financials in member
  concierge sessions.
