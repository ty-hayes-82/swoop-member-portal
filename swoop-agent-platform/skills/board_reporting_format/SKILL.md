# Skill: Board Reporting Format

Structure, tone, and length standards for the monthly board report at
private golf and country clubs.

## Audience

The board consists of elected member-owners (typically 7-12 members). They
are sophisticated, busy, and familiar with the club's operations. They read
the report before the monthly meeting. They skim. Lead with the headline.

## Report Structure

### Page 1: Executive Summary (required)
- **Headline:** One sentence capturing the month. "March: Two members saved,
  $38K protected, service complaints down 18%."
- **3 bullets** — most important operational outcomes, with dollar amounts
- **1 forward-look sentence** — what the team is watching next month

### Page 1-2: Operational Narrative (required)
Sections included only when there is something to report:

- **Member Retention:** Saves by name, intervention type, who approved it, outcome
- **Service Recovery:** Complaint count, resolution rate, any escalated cases
- **Staffing Efficiency:** Recommendations made vs accepted, cost impact
- **Revenue Highlights:** Top 2-3 revenue findings with dollar amounts

### Last Section: Attribution Chain (internal only, not sent to board)
For each claimed outcome: action ID, who proposed it, who approved it,
what was executed, what the outcome was. Never included in board distribution.

## Tone and Voice

- First person as the GM: "We identified," "I called," "We recommended"
- No technical language: no "health scores," "archetypes," "MCP tools," "agents"
- No passive voice: "A call was placed" → "I called James on March 14"
- Board members know the members by name — use them
- Honest about quiet months: "Operations ran smoothly with no major interventions needed."

## Length

- Main report: 1 page for quiet months, 2 pages maximum for active months
- Attribution appendix: separate, internal only

## Numbers Rule

Every number must be traceable. If it cannot be verified from source data,
write "data unavailable." One unverifiable number destroys trust in all numbers.

## Footer Metric (always include)

"Estimated preparation time: [X] hours manual vs. [Y] minutes GM review."
This demonstrates the value of the agent system to the board over time.
