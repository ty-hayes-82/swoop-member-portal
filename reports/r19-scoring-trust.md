## Top 5 Remaining Trust Fixes

### 1. Cite the three market stats on pricing-hero (C → B+)
"3,000+ private clubs," "$2.1B annual dues at risk," and "67% disconnected" appear authoritative but have no source. Add a footnote: "(NGCOA Industry Report 2024 / Club Benchmarking 2024 estimate)" or replace with Swoop's own sourced calculation. Uncited macro stats are the first thing a CFO or board member challenges.

### 2. Surface the data-security FAQ answer by default and add a compliance badge (C+ → B)
"Is my members' data secure?" is collapsed on both the pricing and about FAQ. At the conversion-closest page (pricing), open this answer by default and add a visible SOC 2 Type II / SSL badge or a one-liner like "Data encrypted at rest and in transit. Member PII never shared or sold." Absence of security language at the payment decision point is a material gap for club controllers.

### 3. Source the "65% early-intervention retention rate" calculator assumption (B- → A-)
Every ROI output flows from this single assumption. Add a tooltip or footnote: "Based on 9 of 14 at-risk members retained in the Pinetree CC pilot (Q4 2025)." Naming the source converts a marketing assumption into a real data point and makes the calculator defensible in a board presentation.

### 4. Publish testimonials with full attribution or replace the "(pending)" labels with a consistent disclosure (C+ → B+)
"Name withheld through Q2 2026 pilot" signals honesty but reads as unverifiable to a skeptical buyer. Either (a) accelerate at least one named release before Q2 2026 and use that card as the lead, or (b) rewrite the sub-attribution to "Identity confirmed, name withheld per NDA — full case study Q2 2026" to frame it as a deliberate privacy choice, not an inability to produce proof.

### 5. Fix the stale copyright year in the footer (hygiene, C+ perception)
"©2024 Swoop Staff" appears in footers across all pages. It is now 2026. A stale copyright year is noticed by lawyers, procurement teams, and diligent buyers as a signal of inattention. Update to "©2026 Swoop Staff" or use a dynamic year. This is the cheapest trust fix on the list.
