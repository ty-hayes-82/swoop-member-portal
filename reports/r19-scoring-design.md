## Top 5 Remaining Fixes by Visual Impact

### 1. Mobile homepage: scale heading sizes and fix trust bar wrapping (IMPACT: HIGH)
The mobile full-page screenshot reveals hero and section headings are oversized — the H1 runs 3+ lines at near-full viewport width. Reduce mobile H1 to ~36–40px and cap line-length at 90% viewport. The trust bar items (4 pills) need to wrap into 2×2 on mobile rather than collapsing into a single column with clipped text.

### 2. Tier 3 pricing card: truncate or redesign the long heading (IMPACT: HIGH)
"Signals + Actions + Member App" wraps to three lines in the card header, destroying the visual alignment of all three pricing cards. Shorten to "Pro" or "Full Platform" with the full name as a subtitle in smaller text. This is the most visually broken element in the pricing section.

### 3. Platform and about pages: add a demo CTA close section (IMPACT: MEDIUM-HIGH)
Both platform and about pages end with either FAQ or "Why Not" objection cards and then drop straight to the footer. Home and contact both close with a dark full-bleed demo form section. Platform and about need the same closing section — the current endings are dead-ends with no conversion action.

### 4. Home mobile: dark AI agents panel needs a stacked fallback layout (IMPACT: MEDIUM)
The dark terminal UI with the side-by-side activity feed + agent detail panel is the strongest differentiator on the site, but on mobile it collapses into an unreadable compressed two-column that loses all the detail. Design a single-column stacked version: headline, then the agent detail card full-width, then the mini-card grid below it.

### 5. FAQ sections across all pages: add a visual anchor or section illustration (IMPACT: MEDIUM)
Every FAQ section is a pure white accordion with a plain heading. There is no color, iconography, or imagery to break the visual monotony. Adding even a subtle background tint (the existing cream/sand tone used elsewhere) or a small section illustration would prevent the FAQ from feeling like a dead zone before the footer.
