# CRO Critique — Swoop Marketing Site r18
**Reviewer:** CRO Specialist, B2B SaaS Demo Funnels
**Date:** 2026-04-14
**Focus:** Conversion optimization across demo request funnel — CTA placement, friction reduction, value communication, urgency honesty, dead ends, form design, exit intent capture.

---

## Scoring Legend
- **A** — Best-in-class for B2B SaaS; ship it
- **B** — Solid, minor improvements available
- **C** — Mediocre; leaving conversions on the table
- **D** — Significant CRO problems; fix before next campaign
- **F** — Dead end or actively hurts conversion

# HOME PAGE

## home-hero.png / home-slice-00.png
**Grade: B+**

**Conversion Strengths:**
- Problem-first headline ("None of them talk to each other") creates immediate pain recognition for GMs — one of the strongest openers in golf SaaS.
- Dual-CTA structure (primary "Book the 30-minute walkthrough" + secondary "See it in action") addresses both ready-to-buy and top-of-funnel visitors. The $74K anchor in the subhead creates financial urgency before the ask.

**Conversion Killers:**
- The golf ball/hole image is gorgeous but inert — it communicates the industry without communicating the product. A skeptical GM sees nothing that proves Swoop works.
- "Live under 2 weeks · No rip-and-replace · 28 integrations" trust badges are too small and low-contrast below the CTAs — they're in the exact spot where objections fire and they're barely legible.
- The nav link "Agents" will confuse GMs unfamiliar with AI terminology. It costs cognitive overhead before the hero even loads.

**Fix:**
- Increase trust badge font size by 30%; move them above the CTA row, not below it. Objection-killers should precede the ask.
- Swap the hero image for a real UI screenshot or the dark agent panel — show the product doing something, not a golf ball.
- Rename nav "Agents" to "How It Works" or "AI Engine" for non-technical buyers.

## home-slice-01.png — Social Proof Bar + "Flying Blind" Problem Section
**Grade: B**

**Conversion Strengths:**
- "Founding partner program — Open to first 10 private clubs" in the proof bar is a strong scarcity signal. Real urgency, clearly stated.
- The three problem cards (Member risk blind spot, Complaint follow-up gap, Demand vs. experience disconnect) are excellent pain-point amplifiers with specific, named stories (James Whitfield, 42-minute wait, 6 days unresolved).

**Conversion Killers:**
- "91% confidence" and "84% confidence" badges on the problem cards are developer-think, not buyer-think. A GM doesn't understand confidence scores — they feel like fine print, not proof. They weaken rather than strengthen the narrative.
- No CTA in this section. A highly engaged reader who resonates with the pain cards hits a dead end — they must scroll more to find a CTA. For mobile visitors this is a significant drop-off risk.
- The "300-member Pinetree CC — Live demo environment, real data" badge is excellent but requires a tooltip to explain what it means. GMs may not realize this is their eventual experience.

**Fix:**
- Remove confidence percentages from problem cards. Replace with time-to-detect or dollars-at-risk metrics.
- Add a mid-section CTA button after the three problem cards: "See how Swoop connects these signals →" linking to demo form. This captures resonance at peak pain.

## home-slice-05.png — "Why Not Just..." Objection Handling
**Grade: B**

**Conversion Strengths:**
- Directly naming and dismantling the three most common objections ("Why not a standalone waitlist tool?" / "Why not just use my CRM?" / "Why not build dashboards in Excel?") is excellent CRO practice. Handling objections proactively increases conversion by 15–20% in B2B SaaS.
- Copy is crisp and specific — "A CRM tells you who resigned. Swoop tells you who's about to." is memorable.

**Conversion Killers:**
- After answering all three objections, there is zero CTA. A visitor who has had every objection addressed is primed to convert — this is the highest-intent moment in the entire page and it dumps them into the Agents section with no ask.
- The section reads as "company blog content" visually — three equal cards with no visual hierarchy signal that this is a decision moment.

**Fix:**
- Add a CTA directly below the objection cards: "Every objection answered. One step left — Book the walkthrough →"
- Consider a fourth card that's actually a CTA block: "Still have questions? Talk to a club tech specialist →" with inline email/phone.

## home-slice-09.png — Pricing Preview (from Home)
**Grade: C**

**Conversion Strengths:**
- "Simple pricing. No long-term contracts." directly addresses the #2 B2B SaaS sales objection (commitment fear).
- Three-tier structure with the middle tier highlighted as "Most Popular" is textbook pricing page optimization.

**Conversion Killers:**
- This is a pricing section embedded in the home page *before* the ROI calculator — a visitor sees the price ($499/mo) before they've been shown what ROI they'll get. Price before value is the #1 cause of sticker shock and early exit.
- The $0/mo free tier ("Start on Signals free") is undersold. This is a huge conversion lever — zero-cost entry removes the #1 objection — but it appears as a quiet ghost card compared to the orange "Book the 30-minute walkthrough" on the middle tier.
- The Enterprise tier ("Signals + Actions + Member App") has a secondary "Book the 30-minute walkthrough" button that looks identical in copy to the middle tier CTA. At $1,499/mo, the messaging should acknowledge the price difference and offer a differentiated path.

**Fix:**
- Move the ROI calculator *before* the pricing section — always anchor perceived ROI before anchoring price.
- Add urgency to the free tier CTA: "Start on Signals free — no credit card, live in 48 hours" to reduce friction.
- Differentiate the Enterprise CTA: "Talk to us about full deployment →" rather than the identical walkthrough copy.

## home-slice-11.png — ROI Calculator
**Grade: A-**

**Conversion Strengths:**
- Interactive sliders (Total Members, Avg Annual Dues, Annual Turnover Rate) personalize the calculation — this is exactly the right conversion tool for an audience that thinks in financial terms.
- "13x return on investment" and "$74,012 net revenue gain" against "$5,988 Swoop Pro annual cost" makes the business case immediate and undeniable.

**Conversion Killers:**

- The calculator defaults to 300 members / $8,000 dues / 5% turnover — these are reasonable but should ideally be editable with a "personalize your numbers" label to make GMs feel ownership over the result.

**Fix:**
- Add a prominent CTA *inside* the calculator panel (below the results): "Your club could recover $[calculated amount] — See it in a live walkthrough →" with the calculated number injected dynamically.
- Make the CTA button orange, full-width, and position it at the bottom of the results card so it's visible immediately after the ROI figure renders.

## home-slice-16.png — Demo Request Form (Page Footer CTA)
**Grade: B+**

**Conversion Strengths:**
- "See what your club misses today and can recover tomorrow" is the strongest CTA headline on the site — it creates asymmetric urgency (missing something *today*) while promising fast resolution.
- Four fields (Name, Club, Email, Phone) with an orange "Book Your Demo" button is appropriately lean for a B2B demo request.
- "No credit card required · 30-minute walkthrough · Cancel anytime" reassurance below the button covers the main friction points.

**Conversion Killers:**
- Phone is a required-looking field in a dark-background form — phone number is the #1 form abandonment trigger in B2B SaaS. If it's optional, it should be explicitly labeled optional. If required, it needs justification ("So we can coordinate across time zones").
- The form has no progress indicator or estimated commitment ("Fill this out in 60 seconds" or "We respond within 1 business day").
- "Or email us at demo@swoopgolf.com · (480) 225-8102" is buried in tiny text at the bottom — this is valuable for high-anxiety prospects who want a human path, but it's invisible at current size.

**Fix:**
- Label Phone as "Phone (optional)" or remove it entirely and collect it during the demo scheduling step.
- Add a micro-copy line above the form: "Takes 60 seconds. We respond within 1 business day."
- Increase the email/phone escape valve to 14px and place it more prominently: "Prefer email or a quick call first?" as a labeled section, not fine print.

## home-mobile-full.png — Mobile Home Page (Full Scroll)
**Grade: C+**

**Conversion Strengths:**
- The hero section reads well on mobile — headline, subhead, and dual CTAs are all above the fold.
- The dark agent panel section retains its visual impact on mobile.

**Conversion Killers:**
- The mobile page is extremely long (the full-scroll screenshot shows ~20 distinct sections stacked vertically). There are multiple screens between CTAs — a GM scrolling on mobile will hit a dead end and bounce before ever finding the demo form.
- The ROI calculator sliders will be nearly unusable on mobile — horizontal drag on a small touchscreen is notoriously difficult. If a visitor can't engage with the calculator, the 13x ROI proof never registers.
- The comparison table is likely horizontally truncated on mobile — a four-column table on a 375px screen will either overflow or be illegible.

**Fix:**
- Audit mobile for CTA density: no section should be more than 2 scrollable screens from a CTA.
- Replace the ROI calculator sliders on mobile with a simple input form (number fields for member count and dues) with a "Calculate" button.
- Convert the comparison table to a mobile-friendly accordion or vertical card stack on screens below 640px.
