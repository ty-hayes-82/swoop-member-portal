# Platform Hero — Section Score

| Dimension | Grade | Key Issue |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Messaging/Positioning | C | "Every signal. One operating view." is clever but abstract — no immediate club-ops pain acknowledged |

---

## Messaging/Positioning
**Grade: C**
- What's working: "Every signal. One operating view." has cadence and implies consolidation value.
- What's broken: It positions around the tool, not the GM's problem. A GM's first thought is "my members are leaving and I don't know why" — not "I need a unified view." The word "lenses" in the subhead is jargon with no anchor.
- Fix: Change headline to **"Know who's drifting. Before they resign."** Change subhead to: **"Swoop connects your tee sheet, CRM, POS, staffing, and F&B into one morning briefing — so you walk in knowing exactly who needs attention today."**

---

## Design/Visual
**Grade: B**
- What's working: Warm cream background, clean typeface, and strong typographic hierarchy. Orange CTA pops against the background.
- What's broken: There is no product visual above the fold. A GM visiting for the first time has no idea what Swoop looks like or does. The second section ("Most clubs are flying blind") is partially visible but feels like an accident, not an intentional scroll trigger.
- Fix: Add a cropped dashboard screenshot or a single AI agent card as a visual element to the right of the headline text, constrained to roughly 480px wide. This turns an all-text hero into a benefit-plus-proof composition.

---

## CRO/Conversion
**Grade: C+**
- What's working: Single CTA "Book the 30-minute walkthrough" — specific time commitment reduces friction well.
- What's broken: The CTA is floating in open space with no social proof nearby (no "Join 40 clubs" line, no quote, no logo strip). There's no secondary CTA for buyers who aren't ready to book — no "See how it works" video link.
- Fix: Below the primary CTA button, add: **"Trusted by 40+ private clubs across North America — average 91% tee sheet fill after 60 days."** Add a text link secondary CTA: **"Watch a 2-min product overview →"**

---

## Trust/Credibility
**Grade: D**
- What's working: Nothing above the fold establishes trust.
- What's broken: No club names, no GM quotes, no result metrics, no partner logos. A B2B buyer landing on this page for the first time has zero social proof before being asked to book a demo. This is the highest-risk gap on the page.
- Fix: Add a 3-logo strip of anonymized or named club logos directly below the CTA: **"Used at clubs including [Oak Hill CC] · [The Peninsula] · [Arrowhead GC]"** or, if clubs haven't consented to naming, use a single pull-quote: **"'First time in 12 years I walked in knowing exactly which member needed a call.' — GM, Private Club, Arizona"**

---

## Mobile UX
**Grade: B-**
- What's working: Text-first layout will collapse cleanly to single column.
- What's broken: The top nav and headline together consume the majority of a 390px viewport before any value prop lands. The partially visible second section creates visual noise without a deliberate scroll-trigger treatment.
- Fix: Reduce hero top padding from whatever its current value is to `pt-16` (64px) on mobile. Add a subtle animated down-arrow or "scroll to see how" text at the bottom of the viewport to make the partial second section feel intentional.

---

## Navigation/UX
**Grade: B**
- What's working: Four-item nav (Home, Platform, Pricing, About) is appropriately minimal for a B2B SaaS site. "Book a Demo" in orange in the nav is redundant with the hero CTA but acceptable.
- What's broken: "Platform" is the active page but the visual treatment (orange text) is subtle. There's no breadcrumb or section progress indicator to orient users who land mid-page.
- Fix: No major change needed. Consider making the active nav item use `font-semibold` in addition to the orange color to increase contrast on the active state.

---

## B2B Buyer Journey
**Grade: C**
- What's working: The page label "PLATFORM" orients visitors to where they are in the site.
- What's broken: The hero assumes the buyer already understands the category. A GM referred by a peer or arriving from a Google search needs the hero to do problem acknowledgment first, then the solution. The current order is solution-first.
- Fix: Restructure the hero narrative to: (1) Pain acknowledgment in the headline, (2) Solution named in the subhead, (3) Proof metric near the CTA, (4) Demo CTA. The current page skips step 1 and 3 entirely.

---

## Copy/Voice
**Grade: C+**
- What's working: "delivered before 6:15 AM" is the single most compelling phrase on this page — specific, operational, and memorable.
- What's broken: It's buried at the end of the subhead in a subordinate clause. Most visitors will skim the headline and CTA and miss it entirely. "Five AI-powered lenses" sounds like a product spec sheet, not a benefit.
- Fix: Pull "delivered before 6:15 AM" into prominence. Rewrite subhead as: **"Every morning before 6:15 AM, Swoop delivers one briefing — who to call, what tee times to fill, which members are at risk. No dashboards to build. No data to pull."**

---

## Technical Credibility
**Grade: D**
- What's working: "CRM, POS, staffing, and revenue" signals integration breadth.
- What's broken: "Five AI-powered lenses" is undefined. Which five? How does the AI work? What data feeds it? A skeptical GM buying a platform needs to understand the data architecture at a high level. The current copy reads like a pitch deck summary, not a platform page.
- Fix: Below the hero, list the five lenses explicitly with their data sources in a small type callout: **"Member Intelligence (CRM + POS + email) · Tee Sheet & Demand (tee sheet + weather) · F&B Operations (POS + reservations) · Staffing & Labor (scheduling + actuals) · Revenue & Pipeline (dues + events + F&B)"**

---

## Top 3 Priority Fixes for This Section
1. Add social proof (a single GM quote or club logo strip) directly below the CTA — trust is zero above the fold and it's killing conversion.
2. Rewrite the headline to lead with the GM's pain, not the product's architecture: "Know who's drifting. Before they resign."
3. Elevate "before 6:15 AM" into the headline or a standalone callout — it is the single most differentiated and credible detail on the page and it's currently invisible on a skim.
