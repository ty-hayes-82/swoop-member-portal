## What We Believe

### The club industry has a connection problem, not a data problem.

Clubs have more data than they've ever had. Tee sheet systems track every booking. POS systems log every transaction. CRMs store every member record. Email platforms track every open and click. The data exists.

The problem is that it lives in silos. The tee sheet doesn't know what the POS knows. The POS doesn't know what the email system knows. The CRM doesn't know what any of them know in real time. The result: operators make decisions with fragments of the picture and discover problems weeks too late.

### Cross-domain synthesis is the unlock.

The insight that slow rounds on Hole 12 cause a $31/round drop in dining revenue doesn't exist in the tee sheet system. It doesn't exist in the POS. It only exists when you connect them. That connection — Layer 3 intelligence — is what Swoop provides.

### Intelligence without action is just a dashboard.

Dashboards show you numbers. Swoop shows you what's about to go wrong and gives you a button to fix it. The difference is the action queue: recommended interventions, one-tap approval, human in the loop. We don't just visualize — we operationalize.

### The GM must stay in control.

Club operations are personal. Relationships matter. A system that auto-sends emails to at-risk members or auto-adjusts staffing without approval will never earn trust. Swoop recommends. The GM decides. Always.

### The Layer 3 Position

| Layer | What It Does | Examples | Limitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Layer 1** — Systems of Record | Store and manage operational data | Jonas, ForeTees, Northstar, Club Prophet | Siloed. Each system only sees its own domain. |

| **Layer 3** — Cross-Domain Intelligence | Connect data across all systems to produce insights impossible within any single vendor | **Swoop Golf** | This is us. This is the entire point. |

### Our proprietary insights (examples):

- **$31 per slow round** — connecting pace of play data to dining conversion rates. Neither the tee sheet vendor nor the POS vendor can produce this number.
- **First-domino decay detection** — seeing that email engagement drops precede golf frequency drops, which precede dining drops. No single system spans all three.
- **Staffing-to-revenue correlation** — connecting understaffed shifts to dining conversion loss to dollar impact. Three systems, one insight.
- **Revenue leakage decomposition** — breaking $9,580/month in F&B losses into specific root causes across tee sheet, POS, and scheduling data.
